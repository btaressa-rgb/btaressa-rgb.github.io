Short History of Hypertext

Home > Technical > History Timeline

The NVCC ITE170 student guide to the foundations of the web. This timeline explores the "elastic" history of non-linear information processing. [cite: 2026-02-01]

1945

Vannevar Bush proposes Memex, a conceptual machine to store and link vast amounts of information—the spiritual ancestor of hypertext.

1965

Ted Nelson coins the word "Hypertext" to describe non-linear writing and the interconnectedness of information.

1967

Andy van Dam et al build the Hypertext Editing System and FRESS.

1968

Doug Engelbart demonstrates the NLS (oN-Line System) at the Fall Joint Computer Conference, famously known as the "Mother of All Demos."

1975

ZOG(now KMS) at CMU.

1978

The Aspen Movie Map is created at MIT—the first significant hypermedia videodisc project.

1984

Filevision from Telos hypermedia database for Macintosh.

1985

Symbolics Document Examiner, Janet Walker..

InterMedia, Brown University, N. Meyrowitz.

1986

OWL introduces Guide, first widely available hypertext.

InterMedia, Brown University, N. Meyrowitz..

1987

Apple introduces HyperCard (Bill Atkinson), bringing hypertext concepts to the mainstream personal computing market.
Hypertext '87 Workshop.

1990

The European Conference on HyperText (ECHT) marks a major expansion in the field, setting the stage for global networking.
